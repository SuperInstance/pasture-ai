# SuperInstance Video Creative Brief

## The Two Audiences

**60-second viewer:** Scrolling. Half-paying attention. Has heard "local AI" fifty times this
month and is tired of it. We have one job: make them stop and feel something they
haven't felt before. They are deciding whether to click the 6-minute video.

**6-minute viewer:** They already stopped. They watched the short and felt it. Now they want
to understand it, then *do* something with it. They need to leave the video able to
write their first breed.md and understand why Night School is different from fine-tuning.
They are deciding whether to clone the repo tonight.

---

## The Core Feeling We're Selling

Not features. Not benchmarks. **Ownership.**

The feeling that you have something alive on your hardware that is getting better at
serving you while you sleep — and you can reach in and edit its DNA with a text editor.
The feeling that you will never have to call OpenAI for permission to run your own AI.

Every word, every shot, every cut should push toward that feeling.

---

## What Makes This Different (Honest Assessment)

Most "local AI" projects are: download a model, run a chat interface, done. Nothing evolves.
Nothing specialises. Nothing gets better at *your* tasks.

SuperInstance is different in three concrete ways:
1. **breed.md** — AI personality is a text file you edit like code. Hot-reloads instantly.
2. **Night School** — At 02:00 your agents breed, cull the weak, promote the fit. You wake up
   to a smarter ranch without touching anything.
3. **Species taxonomy** — Not one model doing everything badly. Seven types of agents,
   each optimised for its job (email triage vs. API calls vs. sensor monitoring).

The 60-second video needs to *show* one of these. The 6-minute video needs to make viewers
feel they understand all three and want to build on top of them.

---

## Voice & Tone

- **Confident without being arrogant.** We're not selling snake oil. This runs on real
  hardware, does real things, has a real codebase. Lean into that.
- **Farmer metaphor is load-bearing.** "Ranch", "breed", "cattle", "Night School" — use
  the metaphor consistently. It makes the system intuitive and memorable.
- **Show the terminal.** HN readers, engineers, makers — they trust a terminal more than
  a polished UI. The TUI dashboard is beautiful in its own way. Show it unfiltered.
- **Don't over-promise.** v0.2.0 is not v1.0. The Night School is real but TensorRT-LLM
  full integration is still in progress. The video should be honest about where we are.

---

## What We Explicitly Do NOT Do

- No stock footage of data centers or neural network visualisations
- No voiceover with royalty-free Lo-fi hip hop in the background
- No comparison slides against ChatGPT/Claude — let viewers draw the conclusion
- No "this will change everything" language
- No face on camera required (terminal is the star)

---

## Key Shots/Moments to Capture

1. `superinstance --demo` cold-booting and the TUI appearing
2. Editing `breed.md` in a terminal editor — saving — watching the agent reload in <200ms
3. The overnight Night School results appearing in the log: agents culled, offspring bred
4. The dashboard showing VRAM usage, tok/s, agents active — all real numbers
5. The Jetson hardware itself (optional but powerful — $499 device, full ranch)

---

## Music Direction

No music for the 60-second. Silence + terminal sounds only. The beep of keys. The
satisfaction of a fast compile. If there is any ambient sound, it should be a quiet room
at 02:00 — the time Night School runs.

For the 6-minute, optional: something procedurally generated, slow, patient. Not music
that demands attention. Music that respects that the viewer is thinking.
