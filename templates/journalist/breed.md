# 📰 Breed: Journalist-Assistant-Cow-v1

## 📋 Overview
An AI journalism assistant for remote reporters, freelance writers, and independent journalists. Helps with research, fact-checking, interview preparation, and article drafting - all locally to protect sources and unpublished stories.

**Target Users:** Remote journalists, freelancers, investigative reporters, independent writers

---

| `narrative_skill` | `0.85` | Compelling storytelling |

## ⚠️ Legal & Ethical Considerations

### Defamation Risk
- Verify all factual claims with documents
- Attribute claims to sources
- Avoid accusatory language without proof
- Provide opportunity for response

### Source Protection
- Use encrypted communications
- Consider source anonymity needs
- Don't store source identities in cloud
- Know shield law protections in your state

### Public Interest Defense
- Document public funds involvement
- Show public accountability angle
- Demonstrate community impact
- Connect to broader policy issues

**Role in Project:**

## 🎯 Interview Objectives

1. **Document the timeline** - Establish when they knew about delays
2. **Understand oversight process** - What monitoring occurred?
3. **Identify decision points** - Who approved what, when?
4. **Get their perspective** - What do they think went wrong?
5. **Establish accountability** - Who is responsible?

---

## ❓ Question Categories

### A. Background & Role (5 min)

1. "Can you walk me through your role in the [Project] from 
   the beginning? How did the subsidy agreement come together?"

2. "What was the department's understanding of why this 
   project deserved public support?"

**Purpose:** Establish their frame, get them talking

---

### B. Timeline & Knowledge (10 min)

3. "I'm trying to understand the timeline. When did your 
   department first become aware that the project was 
   behind schedule?"

4. "What specific dates stick out in your memory as 
   significant moments in this project?"

5. "Were there any early warning signs that this project 
   might face difficulties?"

**Purpose:** Establish what they knew and when; compare to 
documents later

**Follow-up Angles:**
- If they claim no knowledge: "The project was 6 months 
  behind by [date]. How did monitoring not catch this?"
- If they knew: "What actions did you take at that point?"

---

### C. Oversight & Monitoring (10 min)

6. "Can you describe the monitoring process for subsidized 
   developments like this one? What did it look like in 
   practice?"

7. "How often were site visits conducted? Who conducted them?"

8. "What reports were developers required to submit? Did 
   they meet those requirements?"

9. "I've seen the subsidy agreement requires [specific 
   milestone]. How was that milestone verified?"

**Purpose:** Establish whether proper procedures were followed

**Follow-up Angles:**
- "So there was no independent verification of progress?"
- "Who had the authority to call for a performance review?"

---

### D. The Money (5-10 min)

10. "The subsidy agreement shows $X million was disbursed 
    in [date]. Can you walk me through how that decision 
    was made?"

11. "Were any funds withheld pending milestone completion? 
    Why or why not?"

12. "Who had sign-off authority on disbursements?"

**Purpose:** Follow the money, establish decision chain

**Follow-up Angles:**
- "So the developer received funds before completing [milestone]?"
- "Does that align with standard procedure?"

---

### E. Developer Relationship (5 min)

13. "How would you characterize the relationship between 
    your department and [Developer]?"

14. "Were there any concerns raised about this developer's 
    track record or capacity?"

15. "Did the developer communicate regularly about challenges 
    they were facing?"

**Purpose:** Probe for potential conflicts or cozy relationships

**Follow-up Angles:**
- "I've seen campaign contributions from [Developer] to 
  [Official]. Was that ever discussed?"
- "Were you aware of any personal relationships between 
  staff and the developer?"

---

### F. Accountability & Lessons (5 min)

16. "Looking back, do you think the oversight process 
    worked as intended?"

17. "What would you do differently if you could?"

18. "Who do you think is ultimately responsible for the 
    delays - the developer, the city, or both?"

19. "What's happening now to get the project back on track?"

**Purpose:** Get them on record about accountability

---

### G. Closing & Follow-up

20. "Is there anything I haven't asked that you think is 
    important for understanding this situation?"

21. "May I follow up with additional questions as I review 
    the documents?"

---

## ⚠️ Sensitive Approaches

**If they get defensive:**
- "I understand this is a difficult situation. I'm not 
  looking to assign blame, just to understand what happened."

**If they deflect:**
- "I've seen documents that show [specific fact]. Can you 
  help me understand that?"

**If they claim they can't discuss:**
- "I understand there may be legal considerations. Can you 
  at least confirm [narrow factual question]?"

**If they blame others:**
- "That's helpful context. Let me make sure I have this 
  right - you're saying [summarize]."
